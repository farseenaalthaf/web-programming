OSCAR

The Oscars, officially the Academy Awards, are a prestigious set of awards presented annually by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS) for excellence in cinematic achievements. The awards recognize accomplishments in filmmaking disciplines such as acting, directing, writing, and cinematography, and are widely considered the highest honor in the film industry. The first ceremony was held on May 16, 1929.
